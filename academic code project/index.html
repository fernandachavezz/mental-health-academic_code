Mental Health & Academic Integrity

Support resources and guidance for upholding the academic code

Supporting Your Academic and Mental Wellbeing

College can be a challenging time, and mental health struggles can sometimes create overwhelming pressures. When stress, anxiety, or other mental health challenges arise, they may cloud judgment, leading some students to consider shortcuts or make decisions that unintentionally violate the academic code. This website aims to provide support and resources to help you manage these challenges, make healthy choices, and succeed without compromising your integrity.

Remember, you’re not alone—there are people and resources here to help you stay on track both academically and personally.

Brown University Academic Code

The Academic Code at Brown University emphasizes the importance of honesty, responsibility, and integrity in all academic work. Students are expected to produce original work, give proper credit when using others' ideas, and collaborate ethically.

Violations of this code, such as plagiarism, unauthorized collaboration, and cheating, undermine the academic community. However, support is available to help you manage pressures that can lead to infractions.

For more details, refer to the full code here on Brown's website.

Mental Health Support Resources

If you’re feeling overwhelmed or stressed, reach out before it impacts your studies. Here are some resources available to you:

People on Campus to Contact

  • Brown University Student Support Services

    Offers guidance and support for students navigating academic, personal, and health-related challenges. Students can meet with a Dean to consult with individual students about their personal questions/concerns.

  • Brown CAPS (Counseling and Psychological Services)

    Confidential counseling services available to all students. CAPS provides therapy, crisis intervention, and mental health support.

  • Office of the Chaplains and Religious Life

    If you’re looking for faith-based support, the Office of the Chaplains and Religious Life at Brown offers guidance and a welcoming community for students of all religious backgrounds.

  • Academic Advisors

    Brown provides access to an array of advisors and support for navigating their academic and personal paths.

National/Non-Brown Specific Mental Health Resources

  • National Institute of Mental Health

    Provides information, resources, and tools for mental health support across the United States.

  • Crisis Text Line

    Text HOME to 741741 for 24/7 support. This line offers immediate help from trained crisis counselors via text message.

Guidance on Avoiding Academic Infractions

Maintaining mental health can help prevent academic infractions. A lot of students on campus often let the following stressors affect their mental health. Try to remember these tips:

General College Stressors

Break Things Down: Focus on one task at a time. Big projects feel more manageable when broken into smaller steps.

Find a Routine That Works: A flexible yet consistent schedule can reduce stress and give structure to your day.

Reach Out: Never hesitate to ask for help. Talk to friends, professors, or advisors if you’re feeling overwhelmed.

Shame

Remember, You're Not Alone: Everyone makes mistakes. Learning from them is part of the journey.

Challenge Your Inner Critic: Instead of harsh self-talk, think about how you would comfort a friend—and show yourself that same compassion.

Talk to Someone: Sharing your feelings with a counselor or friend can help you gain perspective and move forward.

Homesickness/Culture Shock

Stay Connected: Keep in touch with family and friends from home. A quick text can make a difference.

Create New Traditions: Start routines or traditions in your new environment to make it feel more like home.

Explore & Embrace: Seek out familiar places or try new experiences on campus to ease culture shock.

Seasonal Depression

Stay Cozy and Comfortable: Layers are your friend! Always have a scarf, gloves, and a warm coat ready.

Plan for Seasonal Changes: If in a colder climate, try a light therapy lamp or cozy activities to lift your mood.

Stay Active Indoors: Short indoor workouts can help shake off the winter blues.

Burnout

Prioritize Rest: Sometimes rest is the best productivity hack. Listen to your body and recharge.

Take Small Breaks: Step away from the screen every hour to stretch and clear your mind.

Reevaluate Commitments: See if there are responsibilities you can put on hold to lighten your load.

Balancing Being a Student and a Human

Self-Care Is Productive: Eating, sleeping, and mental breaks aren’t just luxuries; they’re necessities.

Limit All-Nighters: Skipping sleep can impact focus and well-being. Try to prioritize rest.

Celebrate Small Wins: Treat yourself when you finish an assignment or have a good study session.

Imposter Syndrome

Acknowledge Your Successes: Reflect on how much you’ve accomplished to reach this point—you’re here for a reason!

Focus on Growth, Not Perfection: Learning is about progress, not perfection. Be kind to yourself.

Find Your Support System: Surround yourself with friends and mentors who uplift you.

The Future

One Step at a Time: Take it one decision, application, or step at a time to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

Use Campus Resources: Career centers, counseling services, and alumni networks can help with career and adulting challenges.

It’s Normal to Feel Uncertain: Most people don’t have everything figured out, so take it easy on yourself.

Course Related Stressors

Talk to Your Professor or TA: They’re there to help! Make use of office hours and ask questions.

Form Study Groups: Studying with peers can make difficult content more manageable and less intimidating.

Give Yourself Credit: Challenging yourself academically is a big deal. Celebrate your perseverance!

Your well-being and academic integrity go hand in hand. Seek support when needed.
